

### Intermediate HTML & CSS Course Feedback Cheat Sheet

| Category                        | Questions to Consider / What to Feedback On | Why This Matters |
|---------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|------------------|
| **Overall Experience**          | How was the course overall?<br>Did you enjoy it? | Helps the team understand student satisfaction |
| **Pacing & Difficulty**         | Was the pace too fast, too slow, or just right?<br>Was the difficulty appropriate? | Ensures future students aren't overwhelmed or bored |
| **Content Quality**             | Were the lessons clear and well-explained?<br>Did the projects help reinforce the concepts? | Identifies which parts are strongest or need improvement |
| **Projects**                    | How did you find the projects (especially the Sign-up Form and Dashboard)?<br>Were they helpful for learning? | Projects are the core learning method in The Odin Project |
| **CSS Grid & Flexbox**          | How comfortable do you feel with Grid and Flexbox now?<br>Which part was most challenging? | These are the main focus of the Intermediate section |
| **Form Handling**               | Did you feel confident with form structure, labels, and validation? | Forms are a critical real-world skill |
| **Most Valuable Part**          | What was the most useful or enjoyable part of the course? | Highlights what’s working well |
| **Most Challenging Part**       | What was the hardest or most confusing part? | Helps improve weak areas |
| **Suggestions for Improvement** | What could be added, removed, or explained better? | Direct input for course evolution |
| **Learning Outcome**            | Do you feel significantly better at HTML & CSS than before starting? | Measures the course’s effectiveness |

### Quick Feedback Tips

- Be honest — both positive and constructive feedback is valuable.
- Mention specific lessons or projects that helped (or didn’t).
- If something was confusing, explain why.
- Celebrate your progress — you’ve come a long way since the Foundations course!
